Good To Know

Questions, answered.

Everything you need to know before we bring the shade to your driveway.

Do you really come to me?
Yes — that's our whole thing. We bring a fully equipped mobile setup to your home, office, or wherever your vehicle is parked. All we need is a little space around the car and access to a power outlet.
How long does a full tint take?
A full vehicle typically takes 2–3 hours depending on the make and how many windows. Single windows or a windshield strip are much quicker — usually under an hour.
How long before I can roll my windows down?
We recommend leaving windows up for 3–5 days while the film cures. You may notice slight haziness or small water pockets at first — this is normal and clears as it dries.
What's the difference between carbon and ceramic?
Both block 99% of UV and never fade or turn purple. Ceramic goes further — significantly more infrared heat rejection for a cooler cabin, with zero interference to phone or GPS signals. It's our most popular choice.
Is window tint legal in my state?
Tint darkness laws vary by state and by window. We'll walk you through the legal limits for your area and recommend a shade that looks great and keeps you compliant.
Is the work guaranteed?
Absolutely. Every install is backed by a lifetime warranty against bubbling, peeling, fading, and discoloration. If anything ever goes wrong with the film, we make it right.
